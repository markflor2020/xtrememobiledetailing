Worth Knowing

Why a detail beats a drive-through wash

The automatic car wash

As many have heard, car washes scratch your paint. Shine a light on the paint of a car that's been through one and you'll see the micro-scratches and swirls — imperfections slowly caused by those harsh machines. It's quicker and cheaper, but is it worth paying hundreds later to correct or repaint?

A professional detailer

A hand wash and hand dry protects your paint in the long run, while maintaining the longevity of the wax or sealant with every visit. It's the difference between preventing damage and slowly causing it.

A side-hustle detailer

What's the difference between a professional detailer and someone doing this as a summer side job? It isn't about the money — it's how they take care of the vehicle. A side-hustler isn't focused on the details, and often uses cheap chemicals that fade plastics over the years, eventually costing your vehicle its true value.

A professional detailer

A professional inspects your vehicle properly and cares for it — careful not to induce more scratches into the paint, and treating the interior so it lasts as long as possible. Sometimes hiring the cheapest "detailer" ends up costing the most in the long run.
